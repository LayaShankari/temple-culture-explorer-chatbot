Brihadeeswara Temple, Thanjavur

Brihadeeswara Temple is a major Chola temple in Thanjavur, Tamil Nadu, dedicated to Lord Shiva. It was commissioned by Rajaraja Chola I in the early eleventh century and is admired for its scale, granite construction, inscriptions, sculpture, and axial planning. The temple is part of the Great Living Chola Temples UNESCO World Heritage group.

The temple is especially known for its tall vimana above the sanctum, the large Nandi pavilion, and its refined Chola bronze and stone traditions. The complex shows the close relationship between royal power, Shaiva devotion, architecture, music, dance, administration, and temple ritual in the Chola period.

Visitors should dress modestly, remove footwear before entering required areas, follow photography rules, and allow enough time to observe the temple from the courtyard. Early morning and evening visits are often more comfortable because the stone courtyard can become hot during the day.
