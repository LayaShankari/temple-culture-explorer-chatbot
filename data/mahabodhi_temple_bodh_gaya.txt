Mahabodhi Temple, Bodh Gaya

Mahabodhi Temple in Bodh Gaya, Bihar, is one of the most important Buddhist pilgrimage sites in the world. It marks the place where Siddhartha Gautama is believed to have attained enlightenment and become the Buddha under the Bodhi tree. The temple complex is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and attracts pilgrims from India, Sri Lanka, Thailand, Tibet, Bhutan, Myanmar, Japan, and many other Buddhist traditions.

The temple has a tall pyramidal tower, sacred railings, shrines, meditation spaces, votive stupas, and the Bodhi tree area. The site represents centuries of Buddhist devotion, artistic development, restoration, and international pilgrimage. It is not a Hindu temple in the usual sense, but it is one of India's most important sacred temple complexes and belongs to the broader cultural landscape of Indian spirituality.

Visitors often meditate, circumambulate, offer lamps or flowers, chant, and sit quietly near the sacred tree. The atmosphere is usually contemplative, with monks and pilgrims from many countries. Respectful silence, modest clothing, and careful movement around meditation areas are important. Photography rules and security procedures should be checked on arrival.
