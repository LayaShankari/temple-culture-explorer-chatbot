Kamakhya Temple, Guwahati

Kamakhya Temple is located on Nilachal Hill in Guwahati, Assam, and is one of the most important Shakti Peethas in India. It is dedicated to Goddess Kamakhya and is strongly associated with Shakta Tantra, fertility symbolism, goddess worship, and the sacred geography of northeast India.

The temple has a distinctive architectural form with a beehive-like dome, sculptural elements, subsidiary shrines, and a sanctum that represents the goddess through a natural stone formation rather than a conventional image. Kamakhya is important for understanding the diversity of Hindu worship, especially traditions centered on the divine feminine.

The Ambubachi Mela is the most famous festival, associated with the annual ritual cycle of the goddess. It draws sadhus, pilgrims, and visitors from many regions. The temple can be very crowded during festival time, and queues may be long. Visitors should be respectful of local ritual practices, dress modestly, and follow temple instructions carefully.
