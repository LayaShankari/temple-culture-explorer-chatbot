Dwarkadhish Temple, Dwarka

Dwarkadhish Temple is a major Krishna temple located in Dwarka, Gujarat, one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites. The temple is dedicated to Lord Krishna as the king of Dwarka and is connected with legends of Krishna's kingdom by the sea. It is also called Jagat Mandir and is central to Vaishnava pilgrimage in western India.

The temple has a tall shikhara, sculpted stonework, flag rituals, mandapas, and a strong relationship with the Gomti riverfront and the Arabian Sea. The temple's flag is changed several times a day in an important ritual act of devotion. Dwarka's sacred landscape includes nearby sites such as Gomti Ghat, Rukmini Devi Temple, and Bet Dwarka.

Festivals such as Janmashtami attract large crowds and devotional celebrations. Daily darshan timings, aarti schedules, and flag rituals structure the visitor experience. Pilgrims should check temple timings, dress modestly, respect queue systems, and plan extra time during festivals and holiday seasons.
