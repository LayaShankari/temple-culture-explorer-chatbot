Siddhivinayak Temple, Mumbai

Siddhivinayak Temple in Mumbai, Maharashtra, is dedicated to Lord Ganesha and is one of the most famous urban temples in India. The temple is known for devotion to Ganesha as the remover of obstacles and the giver of auspicious beginnings. It attracts local devotees, public figures, students, families, and visitors from across India.

The temple has grown from a smaller shrine into a major pilgrimage and urban religious institution. The main deity is a distinctive image of Ganesha with the trunk turned to the right, which is considered especially powerful in many traditions. Tuesday is an especially busy day for darshan, and Ganesh Chaturthi is the most important festival period.

Visitors should expect queues, security checks, and crowd control, especially on Tuesdays and festival days. Offerings may include flowers, coconut, sweets, and modak, depending on temple rules. Modest clothing, patience, and respect for local systems help make the visit smoother.
