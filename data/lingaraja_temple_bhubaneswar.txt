Lingaraja Temple, Bhubaneswar

Lingaraja Temple is one of the most important temples in Bhubaneswar, Odisha, and is dedicated to Harihara, a form combining Shiva and Vishnu associations. It is a major example of the Kalinga style of temple architecture and belongs to Bhubaneswar's historic sacred landscape, which includes many old temples, tanks, and ritual routes.

The temple complex includes a tall rekha deula sanctum tower, jagamohana or assembly hall, natamandira or dance hall, bhoga mandapa, subsidiary shrines, and sculptural ornamentation. The temple shows the mature development of Odishan temple architecture and the relationship between ritual, urban sacred geography, and stone craftsmanship.

Major festivals include Shivaratri and Rukuna Rath Yatra. The nearby Bindu Sagar tank is part of the temple's ritual landscape. Entry rules may restrict non-Hindu visitors from the inner temple complex, so visitors should check current rules and use viewing platforms where available. Respect for local customs, photography restrictions, and conservation boundaries is important.
