Jagannath Temple, Puri

Jagannath Temple in Puri, Odisha, is dedicated to Lord Jagannath, along with Balabhadra and Subhadra. It is one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites and a central institution of Odia religious, cultural, and culinary life. The temple is part of the Kalinga architectural tradition and has a towering sanctum structure, surrounding walls, subsidiary shrines, kitchens, ritual routes, and strong links with the sacred town of Puri.

The temple is famous for its wooden deities, elaborate daily rituals, and Mahaprasad prepared in the temple kitchen. The food offering system is one of the largest and most culturally significant temple kitchen traditions in India. The temple's ritual calendar includes many festivals, each tied to mythology, seasonal cycles, and the relationship between the deity and devotees.

Ratha Yatra is the most famous festival, when the deities travel in large chariots from the main temple to the Gundicha Temple. The event brings the deity into public space and attracts enormous crowds. Entry rules are strict and may restrict non-Hindu visitors from entering the main temple. Visitors should check current rules, respect crowd control, avoid touching ritual objects, and follow instructions from temple authorities.
