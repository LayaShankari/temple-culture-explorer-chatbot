Ramanathaswamy Temple, Rameswaram

Ramanathaswamy Temple is a major Shiva temple located on Rameswaram island in Tamil Nadu. It is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas and is also connected with the Ramayana tradition, where Rama is believed to have worshipped Shiva. Because of this, the temple is important to both Shaiva devotion and Rama-related pilgrimage narratives.

The temple is famous for its long pillared corridors, large gopurams, sacred tanks, sculptural detail, and ritual bathing traditions. Its corridors are among the most admired features of South Indian temple architecture. Pilgrims often perform ritual bathing at wells and water points before darshan, following temple customs and local guidance.

Important practices include darshan of Ramanathaswamy, worship of Parvati as Parvathavardhini, bathing rituals, and visits to nearby Ramayana-associated sites such as Dhanushkodi. Visitors should carry suitable clothing for ritual bathing, follow the order of temple procedures, respect wet floor areas, and check rules on photography and access.
