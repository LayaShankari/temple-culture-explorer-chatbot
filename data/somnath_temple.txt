Somnath Temple, Prabhas Patan

Somnath Temple is a major Shiva temple located at Prabhas Patan near Veraval in Gujarat. It is traditionally counted among the twelve Jyotirlingas and is associated with legends of the moon god Soma worshipping Shiva. The temple stands near the Arabian Sea and is a powerful symbol of pilgrimage, memory, resilience, and sacred geography in western India.

The present temple was reconstructed in the twentieth century in a style inspired by traditional temple architecture, with a prominent shikhara and a dramatic seafront setting. Somnath's history includes cycles of patronage, damage, rebuilding, and cultural remembrance. Because of this, the temple is often discussed not only as a place of worship but also as a site of historical consciousness.

Major observances include daily aarti, special Shiva rituals, Shravan month worship, and Maha Shivaratri celebrations. Visitors can also experience the sea-facing temple precinct and nearby sacred sites connected with Krishna traditions. Modest clothing, security checks, silence in ritual areas, and respect for photography restrictions are expected.
