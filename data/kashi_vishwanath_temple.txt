Kashi Vishwanath Temple, Varanasi

Kashi Vishwanath Temple is one of the most revered Shiva temples in India, located in Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh, on the sacred landscape of the Ganga river. The temple is dedicated to Lord Vishwanath, a form of Shiva as the lord of the universe. It is counted among the twelve Jyotirlingas and is deeply connected with the religious identity of Kashi, one of the oldest continuously inhabited sacred cities in India.

The temple has been rebuilt and patronized across different historical periods. The present structure is especially associated with the patronage of Queen Ahilyabai Holkar of Indore in the eighteenth century. The temple complex has narrow sacred approaches, shrines, ritual spaces, and a strong relationship with nearby ghats, especially the Ganga riverfront where bathing, prayer, cremation rituals, and pilgrimage movement shape daily life.

Important rituals include mangala aarti, bhog aarti, sandhya aarti, shringar rituals, and darshan of the Jyotirlinga. Maha Shivaratri is the most important festival, but Shravan month also attracts large numbers of devotees. Visitors should expect heavy security, strict entry rules, and large crowds. Modest dress, patience in queues, and respect for local ritual movement are important.
