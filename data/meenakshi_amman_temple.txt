Meenakshi Amman Temple, Madurai

Meenakshi Amman Temple is a historic temple complex in Madurai, Tamil Nadu, dedicated to Goddess Meenakshi and Lord Sundareswarar. It is one of the most important examples of living Dravidian temple culture, with towering gopurams, pillared halls, shrines, processional routes, and a strong relationship with the city around it.

The temple is famous for its sculpted gateway towers, the Thousand Pillar Hall, the sacred temple tank known as Porthamarai Kulam, and its annual Chithirai festival. The festival celebrates divine marriage, procession, public ritual, and the cultural identity of Madurai.

Visitors should expect crowds during festival periods and important ritual timings. Modest clothing, silence near shrine areas, respect for queues, and attention to temple rules are important. Some areas may restrict photography or access depending on current temple policy.
