Kedarnath Temple, Uttarakhand

Kedarnath Temple is a revered Shiva temple located in the Garhwal Himalayas of Uttarakhand. It is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas and one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites of Uttarakhand. The temple is dedicated to Lord Kedarnath, a form of Shiva, and is surrounded by high mountains, harsh weather, and a demanding pilgrimage route.

The stone temple is built in a Himalayan setting and is associated with ancient pilgrimage traditions, the Pandava legends, and the sacred geography of the Mandakini river region. Its architecture is simple, strong, and suited to the mountain environment. The temple is open only during the pilgrimage season because winter conditions make the area inaccessible.

Pilgrims usually reach Kedarnath after traveling to Gaurikund and then trekking or using approved transport services depending on availability and rules. Weather can change quickly, so visitors should prepare with warm clothing, rain protection, medical awareness, and official registration where required. The temple is especially significant during opening and closing ceremonies, and the pilgrimage requires physical preparation and respect for fragile mountain ecology.
