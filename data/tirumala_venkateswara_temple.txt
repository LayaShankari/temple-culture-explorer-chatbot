Sri Venkateswara Temple, Tirumala

Sri Venkateswara Temple is located at Tirumala near Tirupati in Andhra Pradesh and is dedicated to Lord Venkateswara, a form of Vishnu. It is one of the most visited pilgrimage temples in the world and stands on the sacred Tirumala hills. The temple is associated with Vaishnava devotion, royal patronage, temple administration, music, vows, offerings, and mass pilgrimage.

The temple follows Dravidian architectural principles with a sanctum, vimana, mandapas, processional spaces, and gold-covered elements. The deity is famous for elaborate alankaram, daily sevas, and the deep emotional devotion of pilgrims. The Tirumala laddu prasadam is especially famous and forms a major part of the temple experience.

Major practices include darshan, hair offering or tonsure, seva participation, prasadam collection, and vows made by devotees. Brahmotsavam is one of the most important festivals, with processions and ritual celebrations. Visitors should book darshan slots when possible, follow queue systems, avoid carrying restricted items, dress traditionally or modestly, and prepare for long waiting times during holidays and festival seasons.
