Golden Temple, Amritsar

The Golden Temple, also called Sri Harmandir Sahib, is the most revered gurdwara in Sikh tradition and is located in Amritsar, Punjab. It is not a Hindu temple, but it is one of India's most significant sacred places and is important for understanding Indian devotional culture, community service, music, and pilgrimage.

The sanctum stands in the middle of the Amrit Sarovar, a sacred pool, and is approached by a causeway. The gilded upper structure, marble work, continuous kirtan, and reflective water create a powerful devotional environment. The complex includes the Akal Takht and a large langar hall where free community meals are served to thousands of visitors daily.

Visitors should cover their head, remove footwear, wash hands and feet where required, and maintain respectful behavior. Listening to kirtan, walking around the sarovar, and participating in langar are central parts of the experience. The site emphasizes humility, equality, service, and devotion. Photography should be done respectfully and in accordance with current rules.
