Virupaksha Temple, Hampi

Virupaksha Temple is located in Hampi, Karnataka, and is dedicated to Lord Shiva as Virupaksha. It is one of the most important living temples within the Hampi UNESCO World Heritage landscape. The temple is linked with the Vijayanagara Empire, older sacred traditions of the Tungabhadra region, and the worship of Shiva and Goddess Pampa.

The temple complex includes a tall gateway tower, courtyards, mandapas, shrines, painted ceilings, sculptural details, and processional spaces. Its setting within the ruins of Hampi connects active worship with royal architecture, market streets, river landscapes, and mythological associations. The temple demonstrates how sacred life continued even after the decline of the Vijayanagara capital.

Important events include the annual chariot festival and marriage festival of Virupaksha and Pampa. Visitors should combine temple darshan with exploration of nearby Hampi monuments, but they should remember that the temple remains an active sacred place. Modest clothing, footwear rules, and respect for worshippers are important.
